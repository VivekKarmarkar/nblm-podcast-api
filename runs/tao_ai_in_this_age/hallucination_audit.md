## Claims NOT subject to formal verification (framing / analogy / non-paper-derived)

Some narrative content is framing, biographical context, or pedagogical analogy and was not put through the skill:

- **Tao's accolades** — "fields medals", "wins fields medals and cracks problems mathematicians have stared at for generations" — independently verifiable biographical fact about the author (he won the Fields Medal in 2006).
- **Tanya Klowden studies art** — paper itself frames the two disciplines as "mathematics and the study of art" so this is grounded indirectly. Tanya is explicitly listed as an author with humanities-side framing throughout.
- **Generic mathematician workflow** ("tries small examples. Plays around. Builds intuition. Notices patterns. Conjectures something general. Tries to break the conjecture. Tries to prove it.") — pedagogical amplification of the paper's "testing out special cases, and then generalizing from those examples" (page 5). Faithful in spirit, slightly expanded for audio rhythm.
- **Closing pep talk** ("Don't drink the vanilla. Don't trust the red team to write your blueprints. And don't forget that the other planets, however interesting, are not where you live.") — clearly framed as the narrator's reflection on the essay, not a claim attributed to the authors.
- **"Court of appeal that actually works"** — narrator's paraphrase of the paper's "rare and precious ability to arrive at a consensus on the validity of any given argument or assertion in the field" (page 7). Same substance, friendlier phrasing.

## Essay-specific risks watched for and NOT detected

The brief flagged two essay-specific hallucination risks that don't apply to experimental papers:
1. **Misattributing positions to Tao that he didn't take.** Watched for, none found. Every philosophical position the narrative assigns to Tao is one he actually argues for in the paper — the decoupling diagnosis, the smell-test framework, the vanilla-extract analogy, the red-team/blue-team distinction, the rejection of all three retreat positions, the Copernican proposal, the cautiously optimistic close. The narrative never claims Tao believes something he doesn't.
2. **Overgeneralizing specific examples into universal claims.** Watched for. One instance ("several problems" instead of "problems") was tightened defensively even though it was a small amplification, not a fabrication. No other instances found.

## Pipeline

The hallucination check was run by:
1. Extracting per-page raw text from `papers/arxiv_2603.26524.pdf` via `~/.claude/skills/reproduce-page-basic/helpers/extract_pdf_text.py` for all 27 pages.
2. For each substantive narrative claim, locating the verbatim supporting excerpt via grep across the per-page `_raw.txt` files.
3. Calling `~/.claude/skills/find-evidence-in-paper/helpers/append_journal_entry.py` to append each verified claim as an indexed journal entry, with the verbatim excerpt, page number, section context, and AI commentary about the evidential relationship.
4. Two pre-emptive narrative tightenings applied (AlphaProof "several" → "problems"; "the most decorated" → "one of the most decorated/accomplished"); both logged above.
